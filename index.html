What is phishing, and how can I avoid it?

Phishing is a type of cyberattack where attackers trick you into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords or credit card details, by pretending to be a trusted entity. To avoid phishing:

  • Verify sender email addresses carefully
  • Never click on suspicious links
  • Look for HTTPS and padlock icons
  • Enable two-factor authentication
  • Use a password manager

How can I secure my online accounts?

Follow these best practices for account security:

Use strong, unique passwords for each account

Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)

Regularly update passwords (every 3-6 months)

Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions

Monitor accounts for suspicious activity

How do I report a cybercrime?

Reporting cybercrime is crucial. Here's how to do it:

Online Portal

File a report at cybercrime.gov.in (India) or your national cybercrime portal

Local Police

Visit your nearest police station with all evidence

Helpline

Call the national cybercrime helpline in your country

What to Include in Your Report:

  • Date and time of incident
  • Description of what happened
  • Screenshots or copies of communications
  • Transaction details (if money was lost)
  • Contact information of involved parties

How can I protect myself from romance scams?

Romance scams are increasingly common. Protect yourself:

Red Flags:

  • Quick declarations of love
  • Refusal to meet in person/video chat
  • Requests for money (emergencies, travel, etc.)
  • Inconsistencies in their stories
  • Professional-looking but stolen photos

Prevention Tips:

  • Reverse image search their photos
  • Never send money to someone you haven't met
  • Be wary of overseas relationships
  • Talk to friends/family about the relationship
  • Report suspicious profiles to the dating platform
